📝 Grammar Points

1. Basic Greeting Structure

In Chinese, greetings follow a simple pattern. The most common greeting uses 好 (hǎo = good).

Person/Title + 好
你好
nǐ hǎo
Hello (to one person you know)
老师好
lǎoshī hǎo
Hello, teacher!
大家好
dàjiā hǎo
Hello, everyone!

2. Plural Suffix 们 (men)

Add 们 after a pronoun or noun to make it plural. It only works with people, not objects.

Person + 们 → plural
你 → 你们
nǐ → nǐmen
you → you all
同学 → 同学们
tóngxué → tóngxuémen
classmate → classmates
老师 → 老师们
lǎoshī → lǎoshīmen
teacher → teachers

3. Honorific 您 (nín)

您 is the respectful form of 你. Use it when speaking to someone older, senior, or in authority.

您 = polite "you"
您好
nín hǎo
Hello (formal, respectful)
谢谢您
xièxie nín
Thank you (respectful)

4. Responding to Thanks

When someone says 谢谢 (thank you), you reply with 不客气 (you're welcome), which literally means "don't be polite".

A: 谢谢！ → B: 不客气！
A: Xièxie! → B: Bú kèqi!
A: Thank you! → B: You're welcome!

🏮 Culture Notes
🙏

Bowing vs Waving

In China, people typically nod or give a slight bow when greeting. A handshake is also common in formal settings. Unlike some Asian cultures, deep bowing is not standard in mainland China.

👩‍🏫

Addressing Teachers

Chinese students always address teachers as 老师 (lǎoshī) or by surname + 老师, e.g. 王老师 (Teacher Wang). It is respectful to stand and say 老师好 when a teacher enters the classroom.

🎭

您 vs 你

Using 您 instead of 你 shows deep respect. It is used with elders, teachers, customers, and people in authority. Young people speaking to each other would normally use 你.

🌏

你好 Around the World

你好 (nǐ hǎo) is understood by over 1.4 billion people worldwide. It is the most recognised Chinese greeting internationally and a perfect starting point for any Mandarin learner!

🕐

Time-Based Greetings

Chinese also has time-specific greetings: 早上好 (zǎoshang hǎo) = Good morning, 下午好 (xiàwǔ hǎo) = Good afternoon, 晚上好 (wǎnshang hǎo) = Good evening.
