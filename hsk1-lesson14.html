Scene 1 — Chinese food dinner at Li Wen's home
李
你们爱吃哪个菜？
Nǐmen ài chī nǎge cài?
Which dish do you like?
白
我喜欢这个，也喜欢那个。
Wǒ xǐhuan zhège, yě xǐhuan nàge.
I like this one, and I also like that one.
陈
这些菜都好吃，还很好看。
Zhèxiē cài dōu hǎochī, hái hěn hǎokàn.
These dishes are all delicious, and they also look very nice.
李

Wǒ ài chī Zhōngguó cài, yě xǐhuan zuò. Dàjiā duō chī diǎnr.

Scene 2 — Planning to fly to Beijing
白
你去过北京吗？
Nǐ qù guò Běijīng ma?
Have you been to Beijing?
李
去过，北京很好玩儿！我去年去的。
Qù guò, Běijīng hěn hǎowánr! Wǒ qùnián qù de.

白
我明年想去。坐飞机去吗？
Wǒ míngnián xiǎng qù. Zuò fēijī qù ma?

Duì. Cóng Dàxīng Jīchǎng fēi.

📝 Grammar Points

1. Coordinate Compound Sentence — ……，也/还……

Two clauses joined by 也 (yě, also) or 还 (hái, also/still) to show that both things are true at the same time.

Clause 1 ，也/还 + Clause 2
我喜欢这个，也喜欢那个。
Wǒ xǐhuan zhège, yě xǐhuan nàge.
I like this one, and I also like that one.
我爱吃中国菜，也喜欢做。
Wǒ ài chī Zhōngguó cài, yě xǐhuan zuò.
I love eating Chinese food and also like cooking it.
这些菜都好吃，还很好看。
Zhèxiē cài dōu hǎochī, hái hěn hǎokàn.
These dishes are all delicious and also look very nice.

2. 爱 (ài) — Love / Like

爱 expresses a strong preference or love for something, stronger than 喜欢. Both can be used with verbs or nouns.

Subject + 爱 + Verb/Noun
我爱吃中国菜。
Wǒ ài chī Zhōngguó cài.
I love eating Chinese food.
你们爱吃哪个菜？
Nǐmen ài chī nǎge cài?
Which dish do you love to eat?

3. 觉得 (juéde) — Feel / Think

觉得 expresses a personal feeling or opinion about something.

Subject + 觉得 + (Noun +) Adjective/Clause
你觉得北京好玩儿吗？
Nǐ juéde Běijīng hǎowánr ma?
Do you think Beijing is fun?
我觉得这个菜非常好吃。
Wǒ juéde zhège cài fēicháng hǎochī.
I think this dish is really delicious.

4. 去年 / 明年 — Last Year / Next Year

Time expressions for past and future years. These work just like 今天/明天 (today/tomorrow) but for years.

我去年去北京了。
Wǒ qùnián qù Běijīng le.
I went to Beijing last year.
我明年想去北京。
Wǒ míngnián xiǎng qù Běijīng.
I want to go to Beijing next year.

🏮 Culture Study — Beijing, Airports & Chinese Hospitality
🏙️

Beijing (北京) — China's Capital

Beijing is China's political, cultural, and educational capital, home to over 21 million people. Famous landmarks include the Great Wall (长城), the Forbidden City (故宫), and Tiananmen Square (天安门广场). It hosted the 2008 Summer Olympics and the 2022 Winter Olympics — the only city to host both.

✈️

Daxing International Airport (大兴机场)

Beijing Daxing International Airport opened in 2019 and is one of the world's largest airports, designed by the late architect Zaha Hadid. Its iconic starfish shape and futuristic design make it a landmark itself. It handles both domestic and international flights alongside the older Capital Airport.

🥢

Chinese Food Culture — 大家多吃点儿!

Encouraging guests to eat more ("大家多吃点儿！") is a hallmark of Chinese hospitality. Hosts feel honored when guests eat well — leaving food untouched can seem impolite. This tradition reflects the Confucian value of generosity and care for others.

❤️

爱 vs 喜欢 — Love vs Like

In Chinese, 爱 (ài) can mean both "love" romantically and "really love/enjoy" for food, hobbies or activities. 喜欢 (xǐhuan) is a slightly milder "like." In everyday speech, people use both — "我爱吃饺子！" means "I absolutely love eating dumplings!"

👨‍👩‍👧

Family Picking Up at the Airport

In Chinese culture, family members commonly go to the airport to pick up (接) returning relatives, even for domestic flights. This shows care and warmth — arriving home to waiting family is a deeply valued tradition, especially after a long journey.

🍜

Regional Chinese Cuisine

China has eight major regional cuisine traditions (八大菜系), each distinct in flavor and technique — from the spicy Sichuan (四川) to the fresh Cantonese (广东) and the rich Beijing duck (北京烤鸭). Sharing a home-cooked Chinese meal (中国菜) with friends, as in the lesson, is one of the warmest social customs.
