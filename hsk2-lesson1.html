📝 Grammar — 语法

1. Plural Suffix 们 (men)

Add 们 to pronouns/people-nouns to make them plural. ONLY for people — never objects!

Person + 们
你 → 你们
nǐ → nǐmen
you → you all
同学 → 同学们
tóngxué → tóngxuémen
classmate → classmates
学生 → 学生们
xuésheng → xuéshengmen
student → students
❌ 您们 WRONG — 您 has no plural. Use 大家 or 老师们 instead.

2. 您 (nín) — Respectful "You"

您 is the honorific form of 你. Use for teachers, elders, superiors.

你 → 您 (when showing respect)
张老师，您好！
Zhāng lǎoshī, nín hǎo!
Hello, Teacher Zhang! (respectful)
谢谢您！
Xièxie nín!
Thank you! (to teacher)

3. Greeting Pattern: [Person/Group] + 好！

Combine who you're greeting + 好. Flexible and productive.

大家好！
Dàjiā hǎo!
Hello everyone! (whole class)
老师好！
Lǎoshī hǎo!
Hello, Teacher! (student to teacher)
同学们好！
Tóngxuémen hǎo!
Hello, classmates! (teacher to class)

4. Thanks & Response: 谢谢 → 不客气

谢谢 → 不客气
谢谢！→ 不客气！
Xièxie! → Bú kèqi!
Thank you! → You're welcome!
谢谢您！→ 没关系。
Xièxie nín! → Méi guānxi.
Thank you (polite)! → It's nothing.

🏮 Culture Notes — 文化
🏫

Chinese Classroom Ritual

In Chinese schools, when the teacher enters, the class monitor calls 起立！(Qǐlì! — Stand up!). Students stand and say 老师好！as a group. The teacher responds 同学们好！then says 请坐！(Qǐng zuò! — Please sit). This ritual still happens in schools across China daily.

🙏

您 — The Art of Respect

Using 您 (nín) shows respect rooted in Confucian values of 礼 (lǐ — propriety). Students must always use 您 with teachers. Using 你 to a teacher is rude. Importantly, 您 cannot be pluralised — this keeps respect personal and deliberate.

🌏

不客气 — Chinese Hospitality

不客气 literally means "don't be polite / don't stand on ceremony." Chinese culture values making guests feel at home — 不要客气！(don't be formal!). The exchange 谢谢 → 不客气 is as natural as "thank you" → "you're welcome" in English.
