📝 Grammar Points

1. Telling Time with 点 and 分

点 (diǎn) = hour, 分 (fēn) = minute. For exact ten minutes, 分 cannot be omitted. For minutes below ten, 零 (líng) must be said.

Number + 点 + Number + 分
九点
jiǔ diǎn
9:00
二十一点十分
èrshíyī diǎn shí fēn
21:10
二十二点零五
èrshí'èr diǎn líng wǔ
22:05 (零 needed for single digit minutes)

2. Modal Particle 吧 — Suggestions

吧 placed at the end of a sentence softens it into a suggestion, consultation, or request.

Statement + 吧
我们下午三点见吧。
Wǒmen xiàwǔ sān diǎn jiàn ba.
Let's meet at 3:00 in the afternoon.
我们去西安饭店吃晚饭吧。
Wǒmen qù Xī'ān Fàndiàn chī wǎnfàn ba.
Let's go to Xi'an Restaurant for dinner.

3. Position of Time Expressions

Adverbs and time expressions usually come before the verb or adjective in Chinese.

Subject + Time + Verb
她上午十点半上课。
Tā shàngwǔ shí diǎn bàn shàngkè.
She has class at 10:30 a.m.

4. Modal Particle 呢 — Confirming a Fact

呢 at the end of a sentence indicates a confirmed, ongoing fact — emphasizing that something is true right now.

Statement + 呢
我明天下午两点还上课呢。
Wǒ míngtiān xiàwǔ liǎng diǎn hái shàngkè ne.
I still have class at 2:00 tomorrow afternoon.
李文晚上还有事呢。
Lǐ Wén wǎnshang hái yǒu shì ne.
Li Wen still has things to do tonight.

🏮 Culture Study — Time & Daily Routine in China
🕗

24-Hour Clock in Formal Contexts

While casual conversation uses 上午/下午/晚上 (morning/afternoon/evening) plus 12-hour time, formal settings like train schedules, official documents, and TV listings in China typically use the 24-hour clock (e.g. 十五点 for 3pm) to avoid ambiguity.

🏢

Standard Chinese Work Hours

Most Chinese offices operate from around 9:00am to 6:00pm, often with a 1-2 hour lunch break — many companies even encourage a short nap (午休, wǔxiū) after lunch, a tradition rooted in maximizing afternoon productivity.

🗣️

The Softening Power of 吧

The particle 吧 is essential to polite Chinese communication — it turns a blunt command into a gentle suggestion. Saying "我们走" (let's go) sounds abrupt, while "我们走吧" sounds warm and collaborative — a small word with big social impact.

🎬

Going to the Movies in China

Cinema-going is a hugely popular pastime in China, with the country boasting one of the world's largest film markets. Tickets are usually booked through apps like Taobao Movie or WeChat, and seat selection is standard — unlike the "first come, first seated" system in some countries.

🏥

Hospital Shifts and Healthcare Workers

Chinese hospitals often operate around the clock, with doctors and nurses working shifts similar to the dialogue's 8:00 start. Public hospitals tend to be extremely busy, and healthcare workers are highly respected for their demanding schedules.

🛍️

Neighborhood Convenience Stores

Small neighborhood shops (店) remain common in Chinese cities despite the rise of supermarkets, offering quick last-minute purchases like vegetables, snacks, and daily essentials — often run by local families who know their customers personally.
