💬 Dialogues
Scene 1 — Talking about the weather
白
今天天气怎么样？
Jīntiān tiānqì zěnmeyàng?
What's the weather like today?
李
今天太冷了！下雪了。
Jīntiān tài lěng le! Xià xuě le.
It's too cold today! It's snowing.
白
昨天呢？
Zuótiān ne?
What about yesterday?
李
昨天下雨了，有点儿冷，不太热。
Zuótiān xià yǔ le, yǒudiǎnr lěng, bú tài rè.
Yesterday it rained, a little cold, not too hot.
Scene 2 — Someone is feeling unwell
陈
你今天怎么了？你看起来不舒服。
Nǐ jīntiān zěnme le? Nǐ kànqǐlai bù shūfu.
What's wrong today? You look unwell.
白
我有点儿不舒服。头疼，可能感冒了。
Wǒ yǒudiǎnr bù shūfu. Tóuténg, kěnéng gǎnmào le.
I feel a little unwell. I have a headache, I might have a cold.
陈
你去看病了吗？
Nǐ qù kànbìng le ma?
Did you go to see a doctor?
白
还没有。多喝热水，多休息就好了。
Hái méiyǒu. Duō hē rè shuǐ, duō xiūxi jiù hǎo le.
Not yet. Drink more hot water and rest — then I'll be fine.
Scene 3 — Following up after illness
王
你现在好一点儿了吗？
Nǐ xiànzài hǎo yìdiǎnr le ma?
Are you feeling a bit better now?
白
好一点儿了，谢谢。天气太冷了，容易生病。
Hǎo yìdiǎnr le, xièxie. Tiānqì tài lěng le, róngyì shēngbìng.
A bit better, thank you. The weather is too cold, easy to get sick.
王
是的，多穿衣服，多喝热水！
Shì de, duō chuān yīfu, duō hē rè shuǐ!
Yes, wear more clothes and drink more hot water!
白
好的，谢谢你。夏天太热了，冬天太冷了！
Hǎo de, xièxie nǐ. Xiàtiān tài rè le, dōngtiān tài lěng le!
OK, thank you! Summer is too hot, winter is too cold!
📝 Grammar Points

1. 太……了 — Too / Extremely

太 + Adjective + 了 expresses that something is excessive or extreme. It's one of the most common exclamation patterns in Chinese.

太 + Adjective + 了！
今天太冷了！
Jīntiān tài lěng le!
It's too cold today!
这个菜太好吃了！
Zhège cài tài hǎochī le!
This dish is incredibly delicious!
夏天太热了。
Xiàtiān tài rè le.
Summer is too hot.

2. 有点儿 vs 一点儿

有点儿 (yǒudiǎnr) comes BEFORE an adjective and implies mild dissatisfaction or complaint. 一点儿 (yìdiǎnr) comes AFTER an adjective and means "a bit more" or "a little" (neutral).

有点儿 + Adj (slight complaint)  |  Adj + 一点儿 (a bit more)
我有点儿不舒服。
Wǒ yǒudiǎnr bù shūfu.
I feel a little unwell. (complaint)
我好一点儿了。
Wǒ hǎo yìdiǎnr le.
I'm a bit better now. (neutral — better by a little)
今天有点儿冷。
Jīntiān yǒudiǎnr lěng.
Today is a bit cold. (slight complaint)

3. Weather Expressions — 下雨 / 下雪

下 (to fall/come down) + 雨 (rain) or 雪 (snow) describes rain or snow falling. Add 了 to show it's happening now or happened.

下雨了 / 下雪了
今天下雨了。
Jīntiān xià yǔ le.
It rained today.
外边下雪了！
Wàibian xià xuě le!
It's snowing outside!

4. 多 + Verb — Do More Of Something

多 (duō, more/many) placed before a verb is a gentle command to do more of that action.

多 + Verb
多喝热水。
Duō hē rè shuǐ.
Drink more hot water.
多休息。
Duō xiūxi.
Rest more.
多穿衣服。
Duō chuān yīfu.
Wear more clothes.

🏮 Culture Study — Weather, Seasons & Chinese Health Wisdom
🌡️

China's Diverse Climate

China has an enormous variety of climates due to its vast size. The northeast (东北) has extremely cold winters with heavy snow, southern cities like Guangzhou stay warm year-round, while Beijing has four distinct seasons — hot summers, cold winters, and famously dramatic sandstorms in spring.

💧

"多喝热水" — China's Universal Health Advice

"Drink more hot water" (多喝热水, duō hē rè shuǐ) is arguably the most famous Chinese health advice — given for everything from colds and headaches to period pain and stress. It comes from traditional Chinese medicine beliefs that warm water aids digestion and blood circulation. Hot water dispensers are found in offices, hospitals and train stations across China.

🧧

Traditional Chinese Medicine (中医)

Traditional Chinese Medicine (中医, zhōngyī) has a 3,000-year history and is still widely practiced alongside Western medicine in China. It teaches that illness often results from imbalance between hot (热) and cold (冷) in the body — which is why weather and diet are closely linked to health in Chinese thinking.

❄️

Four Seasons in Chinese Culture

The four seasons — spring 春 (chūn), summer 夏 (xià), autumn 秋 (qiū), winter 冬 (dōng) — are deeply woven into Chinese literature, art and medicine. Chinese New Year celebrates the beginning of spring, and seasonal eating (eating warm foods in winter, cooling foods in summer) is a key part of traditional health culture.

🏥

Visiting the Doctor (看病) in China

Visiting a doctor (看病) in China typically involves registering at a hospital first (挂号, guàhào), then waiting to see the doctor. Large public hospitals are often very crowded. Many people self-treat minor illnesses like colds with rest, hot water, and traditional remedies before going to a clinic.

🌸

Weather Phrases in Daily Life

Talking about the weather (天气) is a universal icebreaker in China, just as in many cultures. Expressions like "今天太热了！" (So hot today!) or "下雨了！" (It's raining!) are among the most commonly heard phrases in daily Chinese conversation — making Lesson 15 vocabulary extremely practical for real life.
