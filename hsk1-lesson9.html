💬 Dialogues
Scene 1 — Movie plans on campus
李
学校前边有一家电影院。
Xuéxiào qiánbian yǒu yì jiā diànyǐngyuàn.
There is a cinema in front of the school.
白
对。我们晚上去那个电影院看电影吧。
Duì. Wǒmen wǎnshang qù nàge diànyǐngyuàn kàn diànyǐng ba.
Right. Let's go to that cinema tonight to watch a movie.
李
好！我们七点在电影院外边见，好吗？
Hǎo! Wǒmen qī diǎn zài diànyǐngyuàn wàibian jiàn, hǎo ma?
Great! Let's meet outside the cinema at seven, OK?
白
好的，晚上七点见！
Hǎo de, wǎnshang qī diǎn jiàn!
OK! See you at 7:00 this evening!
Scene 2 — Whose book? What are you doing tomorrow?
白
椅子上有一本中文书，那是谁的书？
Yǐzi shàng yǒu yì běn Zhōngwén shū, nà shì shéi de shū?
There is a Chinese book on the chair. Whose book is that?
陈
是我的书，谢谢。这是我的第二本中文书。
Shì wǒ de shū, xièxie. Zhè shì wǒ de dì èr běn Zhōngwén shū.
It's my book, thank you. This is my second Chinese textbook.
白
不客气。你明天上午在哪儿？
Bú kèqi. Nǐ míngtiān shàngwǔ zài nǎr?
You're welcome. Where will you be tomorrow morning?
陈
我明天上午在学校学习。
Wǒ míngtiān shàngwǔ zài xuéxiào xuéxí.
I'll be studying at school tomorrow morning.
Scene 3 — Weekend leisure plans
王
明天星期六，你做什么？
Míngtiān Xīngqīliù, nǐ zuò shénme?
Tomorrow is Saturday. What will you do?
杨
我白天在家里读书，晚上和朋友们去外边唱歌。
Wǒ báitiān zài jiālǐ dúshū, wǎnshang hé péngyoumen qù wàibian chànggē.
I'll read at home during the day, and sing with friends in the evening.
王
你唱歌很好听。您星期六做什么？
Nǐ chànggē hěn hǎotīng. Nín Xīngqīliù zuò shénme?
You sing very well. What will you do on Saturday?
王
我在家里做饭，看电视，和孩子们、小狗玩。
Wǒ zài jiālǐ zuòfàn, kàn diànshì, hé háizimen, xiǎogǒu wán.
I'll cook at home, watch TV, and play with my kids and puppy.
📝 Grammar Points

1. Existential Sentences with 有 and 是

To say something exists at a place, use: Place + 有/是 + Noun. 有 = there is/are; 是 = it is.

Place + 有 + Noun (existence)
学校前边有一家电影院。
Xuéxiào qiánbian yǒu yì jiā diànyǐngyuàn.
There is a cinema in front of the school.
电影院前边是一家超市。
Diànyǐngyuàn qiánbian shì yì jiā chāoshì.
There is a supermarket in front of the cinema. (It is a supermarket.)
桌子上没有小猫。
Zhuōzi shàng méiyǒu xiǎomāo.
There is no kitten on the table. (negative: 没有)

2. Time + Location Word Order

When both time and location are used, time ALWAYS comes first, then location, then the verb.

Subject + Time + Location + Verb
我们七点在电影院外边见。
Wǒmen qī diǎn zài diànyǐngyuàn wàibian jiàn.
We'll meet outside the cinema at seven.
我明天上午在学校学习。
Wǒ míngtiān shàngwǔ zài xuéxiào xuéxí.
I'll study at school tomorrow morning.
安妮下午在家里学中文。
Ānnī xiàwǔ zài jiālǐ xué Zhōngwén.
Annie studies Chinese at home in the afternoon.

3. Ordinal Numbers with 第 (dì)

Add 第 before a number to make it ordinal (first, second, third...).

第 + Number (+ Measure Word + Noun)
第一、第二、第三
dì yī, dì èr, dì sān
first, second, third
这是我的第二本中文书。
Zhè shì wǒ de dì èr běn Zhōngwén shū.
This is my second Chinese textbook.
第一只小狗是我的。
Dì yī zhī xiǎogǒu shì wǒ de.
The first puppy is mine.

🏮 Culture Study — Leisure & Weekend Life in China
🎤

KTV — Singing Culture in China

Going to KTV (Karaoke Television) is one of the most popular leisure activities in China, especially for friends and colleagues. Unlike Western karaoke bars, KTV venues in China offer private rooms where groups can sing together without performing in front of strangers — a much more comfortable and social experience.

📺

Television and Streaming in China

While watching TV (看电视) remains popular, younger Chinese increasingly use streaming apps like iQiyi, Youku, and Tencent Video. These platforms host everything from drama series (dramas, or 电视剧) to variety shows and documentaries — China's own Netflix-style ecosystem.

📚

Reading Culture and Study

Reading (读书/学习) is highly valued in Chinese culture, especially academic study. Students often use weekends to catch up on studying, and there's a strong cultural emphasis on continuous learning throughout life — not just in school years.

🎬

Cinema as a Social Activity

Going to the cinema (电影院) is a major social activity in China, with cinemas located near schools, shopping malls, and residential areas — exactly as described in the lesson. China's box office is one of the world's largest, driven by both domestic films and international blockbusters.

🐶

Pet Culture in Modern China

Pet ownership has surged in China in recent decades — particularly dogs (小狗) and cats. Young urban professionals especially enjoy pets as companions, and a thriving pet industry has developed around food, grooming, accessories and veterinary care.

🏙️

Weekend Plans and Work-Life Balance

The balance between work, study and leisure (休闲) is an evolving topic in China. While the traditional emphasis was on productivity, younger generations increasingly value 周末 (weekends) for personal time, socializing, and self-development — much like the characters in the lesson dialogue.
