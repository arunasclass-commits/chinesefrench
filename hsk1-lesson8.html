💬 Dialogues
Scene 1 — A kitten visit
白
房间外有一只小猫。
Fángjiān wài yǒu yì zhī xiǎomāo.
There is a kitten outside the room.
陈
我没看见，它在哪儿呢？
Wǒ méi kànjiàn, tā zài nǎr ne?
I didn't see it. Where is it?
白
它在桌子下呢。
Tā zài zhuōzi xià ne.
It is under the table.
陈
这只小猫真漂亮！
Zhè zhī xiǎomāo zhēn piàoliang!
This kitten is really beautiful!
Scene 2 — Making plans to meet
白
我们在哪儿见呢？
Wǒmen zài nǎr jiàn ne?
Where shall we meet?
李
在学校书店前见吧。
Zài xuéxiào shūdiàn qián jiàn ba.
Let's meet in front of the school bookstore.
白
好的。下午两点你能到吗？
Hǎo de. Xiàwǔ liǎng diǎn nǐ néng dào ma?
Okay. Can you arrive at 2:00 in the afternoon?
李
我能到。我在学校吃午饭。
Wǒ néng dào. Wǒ zài xuéxiào chī wǔfàn.
Yes. I'll have lunch at school.
Scene 3 — At the hospital with Dr. Hu
刘
小胡，还没吃饭呢？
Xiǎo Hú, hái méi chī fàn ne?
Xiao Hu, haven't you eaten yet?
胡
没吃呢。大医院病人多，医生非常忙。
Méi chī ne. Dà yīyuàn bìngrén duō, yīshēng fēicháng máng.
Not yet. Large hospitals have many patients, doctors are very busy.
刘
我爸爸也在医院工作，他也非常忙。
Wǒ bàba yě zài yīyuàn gōngzuò, tā yě fēicháng máng.
My father also works at a hospital, he's also very busy.
胡
你家有两个医生？
Nǐ jiā yǒu liǎng gè yīshēng?
There are two doctors in your family?
刘
对。
Duì.
Yes.
📝 Grammar Points

1. Positional Words (方位词)

Chinese uses positional words to describe location: 上 (on), 下 (under), 里 (inside), 外 (outside), 前 (in front of), 后 (behind).

Noun + Positional Word
桌子上 / 桌子下
zhuōzi shàng / zhuōzi xià
on the table / under the table
学校前 / 学校后
xuéxiào qián / xuéxiào hòu
in front of school / behind school
房间里 / 房间外
fángjiān lǐ / fángjiān wài
inside the room / outside the room

2. Preposition 在 (zài) — Location of Action

在 combines with a location word and is placed before the verb to show where an action happens.

Subject + 在 + Place + Verb + Object
我在学校吃午饭。
Wǒ zài xuéxiào chī wǔfàn.
I have lunch at school.
他爸爸在医院工作。
Tā bàba zài yīyuàn gōngzuò.
His father works at a hospital.

3. Modal Verb 能 (néng) — Ability

能 placed before a verb expresses ability, possibility, or permission.

Subject + 能 + Verb
下午两点你能到吗？
Xiàwǔ liǎng diǎn nǐ néng dào ma?
Can you arrive at 2:00 p.m.?
我不能去学校吃午饭。
Wǒ bù néng qù xuéxiào chī wǔfàn.
I can't go to school for lunch. (negative: 不能)

4. Measure Words + Demonstratives

Measure words can combine directly with demonstrative pronouns like 这 (this) and 那 (that).

这/那 + Measure Word + Noun
这只猫
zhè zhī māo
this cat
那个人
nà gè rén
that person

🏮 Culture Study — Hospitals, Locations & Friendly Names in China
🏥

China's Crowded Hospitals

Large public hospitals (大医院) in China are often extremely busy due to the sheer population size — millions of patients visit major city hospitals each year. This is why the dialogue mentions "病人多，医生非常忙" (many patients, doctors very busy) — it reflects a very real daily reality.

👨‍👩‍👧

"小 + Surname" — A Sign of Closeness

Adding 小 (xiǎo, "little") before someone's surname — like 小胡 (Xiao Hu) — is a warm, informal way to address younger colleagues or close friends. It signals familiarity and affection, similar to using a nickname in English.

📍

Meeting at Landmarks

Because Chinese cities can be vast and addresses complex, people often arrange to meet at recognizable landmarks (like "in front of the bookstore") rather than precise street addresses — a practical habit that makes coordinating much easier.

🐱

Cats in Chinese Culture

Cats are popular pets in modern China and are seen as symbols of independence and good fortune in some traditions. Stray cats are also commonly cared for by communities, especially around university campuses and residential complexes.

👨‍⚕️

Respect for the Medical Profession

Doctors (医生) hold a position of high respect in Chinese society, similar to teachers. Many families take pride in having multiple generations work in medicine, as mentioned in the dialogue about two doctors in one family.

🍱

Lunch at School or Work

Eating lunch (午饭) at school or work canteens is very common in China, rather than going out. Many schools and workplaces provide affordable cafeteria meals, making "I have lunch at school" (我在学校吃午饭) a very typical daily statement.
